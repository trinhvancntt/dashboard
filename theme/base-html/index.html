Our Action

Cultural heritage preservation

Our hotel, housed in the former Kiyomizu Elementary School built in 1933, is dedicated to preserving and revitalizing this historically and culturally significant building for future generations. Within the facility, approximately 280 pieces of art are displayed, promoting a harmonious coexistence of cultural heritage and new creative expression.

SDG 11 SDG 12

Preservation of Japanese culture

Through exclusive experiences such as tea ceremony sessions and maiko performances offered only to guests, we provide opportunities to engage with traditional Japanese culture and help pass its charm on to future generations. At the on-site SUSHI-BAR, we continue the legacy of Japanese culinary culture into the future.

SDG 9 SDG 11 SDG 17

Efforts to reduce plastic waste

We are actively working to reduce plastic waste by switching the drinking water in guest rooms to aluminum bottles and introducing eco-friendly amenities such as wooden brushes.

SDG 7 SDG 12 SDG 13 SDG 14 SDG 15

Promoting the use of local ingredients

By incorporating locally sourced ingredients grown by regional producers and traditional local cuisine into our menu, we aim to preserve and promote the region’s food culture while contributing to the revitalization of the local community.

SDG 3 SDG 12 SDG 14 SDG 15

Plant-based menu

As part of our efforts to accommodate dietary diversity, we offer vegan menus made with plant-based ingredients. Even sushi and burgers are available in vegan options, ensuring an inclusive dining experience where everyone can enjoy their meal with peace of mind.

SDG 3 SDG 10 SDG 12 SDG 16
